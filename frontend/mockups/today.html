Factor-label vocabulary
Same four-factor language on every RRG table across the platform — Global indices, India sectors, Explorer, instrument detail. Read once here, recognise everywhere.
→ Full methodology
RS · Relative Strength
Asset's return vs benchmark, standardised (z-score over 60d). Four lenses: MSCI World · S&P 500 · Nifty 50 · Gold. RRG quadrant follows RS level × momentum.
LEADINGRS > +0.5, momentum up
IMPROVRS rising, still < +0.5
WEAKRS falling, still > 0
LAGGINGRS < −0.5, momentum down
Momentum
Rate of change of RS (Δ over 10d). Answers is this getting better or worse? — independent of absolute level.
ACCELΔRS > +0.5 · getting stronger
STALL|ΔRS| ≤ 0.5 · flat
DECELΔRS < −0.5 · losing steam
Volume
Vol ratio = today ÷ 20-day avg. Label combines ratio with OBV direction on closes-up vs closes-down days.
ACCUM> 1.2× & OBV↑ on up-days
NEUT0.85–1.2× · no OBV tilt
DISTRIB< 0.85× or OBV↓ on down-days
Breadth
% of constituents above 50-DMA (for sectors: member stocks; for indices: component stocks). Tells you if the move is broad or concentrated.
EXPAND≥ 60% · broad participation
NARROW40–60% · split tape
THIN< 40% · few leaders carrying
Div / Conv
How many of the 4 factors agree. Divergence is called out loudly with the dissenting factor named — that's where the risk or the opportunity hides.
CONV 4/4all 4 bullish
CONV 3/43 agree bullish, 1 neutral
DIV 2/4factors disagree — investigate
CONV 4/4Rall 4 bearish
