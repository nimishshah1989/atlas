Methodology — Market Breadth Indicators

Show / hide
Market breadth measures how many stocks participate in a trend, not just where the index is. An index can rise while fewer and fewer stocks participate — a warning of concentrated, fragile leadership. Breadth analysis captures this hidden deterioration.

21-EMA count — the number of Nifty 500 stocks closing above their 21-day exponential moving average. Because the EMA weights recent prices more heavily, this is the most responsive breadth measure. Above 60% (300 stocks) indicates short-term positive market internals.

50-DMA count — stocks above their 50-day simple moving average. The 50-DMA represents the intermediate trend. Sustained above 50% (250 stocks) across multiple weeks is a healthy sign for medium-term holding periods.

200-DMA count — stocks above their 200-day simple moving average. The primary trend indicator. Readings below 40% (200 stocks) have historically preceded meaningful corrections. This is the signal most relevant for long-term allocation decisions.

Zone definitions follow Zweig Breadth Thrust and O'Neil institutional consensus: Overbought ≥80% (≥400/500), Oversold ≤20% (≤100/500). These represent statistical extremes in the historical distribution of breadth readings. 
Breadth_pct = (count_above_MA / universe_size) × 100

Zone classification:
  OB  if count >= 400  (>=80%)   → systematic trim signal
  OS  if count <= 100  (<=20%)   → systematic buy/redeploy signal
  Neutral otherwise              → SIP continues normally
