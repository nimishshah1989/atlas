The 5-surface IA

Top nav is the primary navigation on every page and every shell. Five items, in FM-workflow order: Today (watch) → Explore (scan + drill) → Decide (execute) → Lab (evidence) → Report (track). No second-level menu in the top bar — second level lives in the sub-bar below, scoped to the active surface.

The sub-bar pattern

Every surface has a sub-bar at the top that does two things: (1) anchors or sub-pages within the surface on the left, (2) universe + sync + refresh on the right. On Today it is anchor-links to the bands (Global briefing, India briefing, Alerts, Yesterday's scorecard). On Explore it is the drill-level router (Global / Country / Sector / Instrument). On Decide / Lab / Report it is the sub-page list. Sub-bar is sticky below the top-bar so navigation stays in view while you scroll the dense body.

The page-header pattern

Page header is always: eyebrow · serif title · one-sentence sub · global + India regime chips on the right. The two regime chips are present on every page, not just Today — because every analytical read is conditional on the regime. Clicking a chip opens that regime's explainer drawer. This is the §12 pattern made global.

Breadcrumb rule

Breadcrumb renders only on drill pages — Explore country / sector / instrument, Decide portfolio-detail, Lab run-detail. Top-level surfaces (Today, Explore-global landing, Decide home, Lab home, Report home) have no breadcrumb; the top-nav active state is the location indicator. Rule of thumb: if the URL has more than one meaningful segment past the surface name, render a breadcrumb.

Universe pill

Today is India-first always — the global band exists to contextualise India, not to compete with it. Changing universe (to US, China, etc.) routes you into Explore at the country level, where the country itself is the subject. Keeping Today locked to India prevents the page from becoming "global dashboard for nothing in particular."
